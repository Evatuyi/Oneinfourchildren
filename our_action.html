Our 1 million Books Of Hope Campaign

Donate Books


1 in 3 in South Sudan is Illiterate. Between 2011 and 2015 than 100 schools were completely ravaged by targeted aerial bombardments and ground. Today, many classrooms are still conducted under trees and in dilapidated classrooms, Schools and teachers have no access to books with which to teach.

In response this urgent need we want to Donate 1 million books to schools in the region in 5 years By Donating books to us, you are supporting our work to eradicate illiteracy and poverty in the Nuba Mountains, surrounding Regions and Africa as a whole. We are Setting up new up schools and libraries to bring education and knowledge to more people.

Why Donate Books


Books improve community literacy standards. We run volunteer led reading and writing programs to improve community literacy standards

Donating Books will mean a reduced student teacher ratio and promote equitable access to learning resources removing distance to education and other home constraints.

More books Promote equal and equitable access to learning resources ranging. knowledge gained from reading books will empower communities to self innovate and fight hunger and poverty

Donating used books to us, is not only environmentally friendly, it means freeing up more space for more important things

Books provide an opportunity to create an environment where students can read for pleasure, enrich their intellectual curiosity and enhance emotional growth.

Hope Library Project

Games & Sports


This program is designed to teach the value of teamwork and empathy. They build cohesion, life skills like self-awareness, problem solving. It also creates a fun environment to connect with peers and share anecdotal experiences, stories and ideas. This is an important aspect of the healing process for migrants


Culture


Culture is an important aspect of individual and collective identity. Our program offers safe spaces for refugees and migrants to participate in various art forms like poetry and storytelling, dance, singing, music, drawing, painting and theater. We teach the youth to learn how to express their emotions in new and creative ways. Skills learned such as decision-making, communication, self-awareness and problem solving empower the youth and enable them to have a voice.

Donate Funds

Donate Funds to Us


Funding is the backbone of any Not for profit organisation. Ours is no different. Shipping Books to Africa is immensley expensive. Funds are vital for creating a fully functional and thriving ecosystem.

Why Donate to US


Primarily we use donated funds to;

Provide and facilitate youth programs and services in Australia that allow us to educate, empower and advocate for African migrants and refugees

Facilitate collection and storage of the books in Australia until they are ready for Shipping

Transport the books to the Nuba mountains

Pay for transportation costs to get the books to school libraries in the Nuba Mountains

Pay library staff and volunteers nominal wages to manage and maintain the library

Build libraries and fund programs that enhance the learning experience of the students

Build Capacity to enable us reach more people in need of education.

Donate Your Time

Youth Led


We assist the youth to develop critical thinking through self advocacy, community advocacy and societal advocacy training. They gain leadership skills, interpersonal and digital skills to tackle some of the biggest issues affecting them. We bring together policy advocates, thought leaders, change makers, and some of the sharpest minds in today's world to tackle some of the most pressing issues affecting refugees and migrants. By sharing their experiences young people are able to bring to bear issues that matter to them.


Youth Led

One in four children Inc. has a vision to make knowledge accessible to all who seek, this is supported by our mission to eradicate poverty by building a future where all children can read and write through education and knowledge sharing. We work with government and private organisations to focus efforts on.


(1)

Restoring the dignity of children by providing access to sustainable education and access to quality schooling for all children, as per article 26 of the human rights declaration;

  • Collecting and donating recycled books, school materials and technology to support learning.

  • Creating centres of excellence for modern education through personalised partnership with mission schools and organisations.

    • (2)

    Maintaining a commitment to championing and addressing inequality and inequity in African schools by;


  • By addressing unequal and inequitable student needs and backgrounds.

  • Working with government to fund programs aimed at early childhood education and reducing distance to education centres, building physical and digital learning spaces.

  • Participating in policy formulation and decision making with government bodies.

  • Providing guidance on modern curriculum development in collaboration with Academia, the private sector and other partner organisations in the regions we work.

    • (3)

    Supporting sustainable capacity building in Africa through;


  • investment in quality teacher education, and community employment schemes.

  • Supporting community led infrastructure development and entrepreneurship programs aimed at creating safe spaces, restoring peace, promoting health and wellbeing, and nutrition for school children and their families.

  • supporting peer to peer learning programs within schools in the Nuba Mountains, Sudan and Africa.

    • (4)

    Tenaciously and rigorously speaking up for refugees and migrant rights, as well as defending instances of child abuse wherever they occur within the African continent, and globally.


    (5)

    Promoting and celebrating heritage, identity and African culture with the wider international community through Art, storytelling and performance arts.
